# Seed-echo outreach — 30 founding members for launch

The goal: launch Echo with **30 great echoes from 30 real people** so the first 100 visitors see a populated feed instead of "Nothing to show yet." This is the single highest-leverage thing you can do in the week before launch.

## Why 30

- Below 15 = the For You section feels empty after one swipe.
- 30 covers the first 3-4 cards in every default scroll on every device.
- More than 50 is diminishing returns — and unreachable in a week without sounding desperate.

## Who to ask

Aim for **variety over volume**. Mix:

- 5 founders / indie hackers (writes from experience, audience overlap with Echo's target)
- 5 designers / writers (will produce visually + verbally strong echoes)
- 5 builders / engineers (technical takes that earn respect)
- 5 personal-essayist types (the "thinks out loud" archetype)
- 5 close friends (will actually do it for you)
- 5 wildcards — a chef, a teacher, a therapist, a musician, a parent (variety reads as "this isn't just for tech bros")

**Don't ask:**
- Strangers
- People with under 500 followers anywhere (they'll feel undervalued)
- Anyone whose voice you don't already know well — bad echoes drag down the average

## The ask itself

People say yes if the ask is **specific, scoped, and reciprocal**. 90 seconds of their time, with a clear visible benefit, and a token of effort from you (you've already written them a question they'd want to answer).

### Template — DM / iMessage / WhatsApp (preferred)

```
Hey [Name] — quick favor.

I'm launching Echo next week. It's a social app where every post is two parts: a question that sparked it + your take.

I'd love one echo from you to seed the feed. I picked a question I thought you'd actually have something to say on:

>> [PICK ONE FROM THE PROMPT LIBRARY BELOW, TAILORED TO THEIR INTERESTS] <<

90 seconds tops. TestFlight link if you're game:
[TESTFLIGHT LINK]

If it's not your vibe, totally fine — saying no doesn't hurt my feelings.

— [Your Name]
```

Subject: `Quick favor — 90 seconds for Echo launch?`


## Prompt library — match to recipient

Pick ONE per person. The point is they think "oh, I actually have a take on that" within 5 seconds of reading the message. Bad prompts get ignored; specific prompts get answers.

### For founders / indie hackers

- What's the most expensive thing you've ever changed your mind about?
- What did you believe about your industry at 22 that you'd argue against today?
- What's a piece of advice you ignored that turned out to be right?
- What's the difference between the company you set out to build and the one you're actually building?
- What's a metric you used to chase that you now think was a distraction?

### For designers

- What's a piece of design — UI, object, signage — you love and never tire of?
- What's the most underrated principle in your craft?
- What's a design decision you saw on a product nobody else noticed?
- What did you learn from the worst design feedback you've received?
- What's a design you wish you'd made?

### For writers / essayists

- What's a sentence you've read this year that you can't forget?
- What's an essay that lives rent-free in your head?
- What's a writing rule you keep breaking?
- What's the book you give as a gift more than once?
- What's a question you wish more interviewers asked you?

### For engineers

- What's a bug you remember years later, and what did it teach you?
- What's the most useful question you ask in a code review?
- What did you learn from the worst codebase you've worked in?
- What's an old idea in computing that's still ahead of where we are now?
- What's a tool you'd build for yourself if you had a free week?

### For personal-essayist types

- What's something that took you embarrassingly long to learn?
- What did you outgrow in the last year?
- What's a moment you knew you were no longer the same person?
- What's a small ritual that anchors your week?
- What's a compliment you got that you keep replaying?

### For close friends (you know what they think about — just ask)

Just write them a one-liner: "Hey, can you answer one of these on Echo?" and pick whatever fits their voice. They'll do it because it's you.

### Wildcards (chef / teacher / therapist / musician / parent)

- What's something everyone in your field knows that nobody outside it does?
- What's a moment that made you sure you were in the right line of work?
- What's a question your students / patients / kids / customers ask that you wish they didn't have to?
- What's a piece of your craft that doesn't show up in the finished thing?

## The day-of process

1. **The morning of launch**, send all 30 messages at once. Friends won't get FOMO if they all land in the same hour.
2. **Don't follow up**. If they don't reply within 48 hours, they're not going to. Move on.
3. **Reply to every published echo personally**, in the app, within 6 hours. Reaction + comment. This signals to the seeders that their post mattered.
4. **Track in a spreadsheet**: name → asked? → published? → URL. Aim for 20+ published from 30 asked.

## The "thank you" follow-up (1 week post-launch)

```
Hey [Name],

Quick note — your echo got [N] reactions and [N] reads. It seeded the feed at exactly the right moment. Genuinely grateful.

[Optional: any user signal that ties to their post — "@someone quoted yours in their reply" / "X downloaded after sharing it"]

Drinks on me next time we're in the same city.

— [Your Name]
```

This is the loop. Founding members who feel seen will tell their networks. The next 100 users come from them.

## Anti-patterns

Don't:

- Mass-send the same message to 30 people (people can tell, it cheapens the ask)
- Reuse the same prompt across multiple recipients (they'll compare notes and the "I picked one for you" framing collapses)
- Send after launch — pre-launch energy is the best leverage you'll have
- Promise anything specific in return (this is a favor, not a transaction)
- DM people who don't follow you back on the relevant platform (you're a stranger to them)
- Ask for retweets / reposts / "spread the word" in the same message (one ask per email)

## How this scales

If you get 20 of 30 to publish, and each seeder shares back to their own audience even once, you've created a 600-2000 person introduction surface. **That's the actual launch.**

Hitting 10K/week isn't about one giant moment — it's about 20 small ones that compound.
